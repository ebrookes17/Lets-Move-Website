Let's Move offers three different kinds of yoga: Kundalini, Vinyasa, and Yin.

Kundalini Yoga

Kundalini is a form of yoga that focuses on the transformation of one's own consciousness. Energy centers called Chakras which run through the spine are awakened in Kundalini yoga. This activation comes through a disciplined practice of Pranayama (breathing exercises), Bhandas (body locks), Kriyas (exercise sets), Asanas (postures), Mudras (gestures), and Mantras (sacred sounds). Kundalini classes are not only an intensive physical practice, but also a test of one's control over their awareness.

Our Kundalini yoga instructor: Arif Chandra

Vinyasa Yoga

Commonly referred to as "flow" yoga, Vinyasa places special emphasis on moving through a series of poses in tandem with the breath. Each pose in Vinyasa is often held only for a short time (the duration of an inhale or exhale, or several) while the poses are linked together in a fluid way. "Sun salutations" are certain sequences in Vinyasa yoga that help energize the body and mind and are typically performed in the morning. Let's Move offers a Vinyasa class at 8 A.M. every day of the week (except for sunday) for this reason.

Our Vinyasa yoga instructor: Pranita Dhaliwal

Yin Yoga

In Yin yoga, emphasis is placed on more space for one to breathe and relax, with poses being held for several minutes. These longer holds allow one to be still, be present, and simply feel through every movement and stretch. Deep connective tissues, bones, joints, fascia, and ligaments are given a chance to recover in this soothing experience. A Yin class can be the perfect remedy after a busy week or an intense work-out.

Our Yin yoga instructor: Holly Quinn
